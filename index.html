How Prediction Markets Work

A simple, four-step process from event creation to settlement. Prices reflect probability, from 1% to 99%.

1. Event Definition

An event with a clear, verifiable outcome is created. For example, "Will India's GDP growth exceed 7% in 2026?".

2. Trading Contracts

Users buy 'Yes' or 'No' shares. A 'Yes' share pays $1 if the event occurs, $0 otherwise. Share prices act as probabilities.

3. Price = Probability

A share price of $0.65 for 'Yes' implies a 65% market-consensus probability that the event will happen.

4. Settlement

Once the outcome is known, winning shares pay out $1 each. Your profit is the payout minus your initial investment.

Example Transactions

See how your knowledge can translate into profit with these hypothetical trades.

"Will Apple release an AI-powered iPhone by June 2025?"

Current Market Price (Yes) $0.78

Your Prediction: You believe the market is underestimating the probability.

  • 1. You Buy: 100 'Yes' shares at $0.78 each.
  • 2. Your Investment: $78.00
  • 3. The Outcome: Apple announces the AI iPhone in May 2025.
  • 4. Your Payout: Your 100 shares are now worth $1 each, totaling $100.
  • 5. Your Profit: $100 (Payout) - $78 (Investment) = $22.00

"Will SpaceX land Starship on Mars by 2030?"

Current Market Price (Yes) $0.22

Your Prediction: You believe the market is overestimating the probability.

  • 1. You Buy: 200 'No' shares. The price for 'No' is ($1 - $0.22) = $0.78 each.
  • 2. Your Investment: $156.00 (200 * $0.78)
  • 3. The Outcome: By 2030, no landing has occurred. The 'Yes' outcome is false.
  • 4. Your Payout: Your 200 'No' shares are now worth $1 each, totaling $200.
  • 5. Your Profit: $200 (Payout) - $156 (Investment) = $44.00
